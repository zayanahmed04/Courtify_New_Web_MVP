# Admin Dashboard and Management

## Overview of Admin Responsibilities

The admin team oversees the entire Courtify platform, ensuring quality, safety, and compliance. The admin dashboard provides comprehensive tools for managing users, approving facilities, monitoring activities, and maintaining platform integrity. This guide covers all admin functions.

## Accessing the Admin Dashboard

Admin users log in with their admin credentials and access the "Admin Dashboard." This centralized hub displays:
- Platform statistics and overview
- Pending court approvals
- User management section
- Activity logs
- System reports
- Settings and configuration

## Key Admin Responsibilities

### 1. Approving New Courts
When court owners register facilities, they appear in the approval queue.

**Review Process**:
- Verify court owner is legitimate
- Check court information accuracy
- Review submitted photos
- Confirm location is valid
- Ensure pricing is reasonable
- Verify all required fields are complete

**Actions**:
- **Approve**: Court becomes visible to players immediately
- **Reject**: Court not listed; owner notified with reason
- **Request Changes**: Ask owner to provide more information or fix issues

### 2. Managing User Accounts
Admins monitor and manage all user accounts to maintain platform integrity.

**User Types**:
- Regular players
- Court owners
- Other admin staff

**Management Options**:
- View user profiles and activity
- Disable accounts (if violation of terms)
- Delete user accounts (if requested or violating policies)
- Review user complaints and disputes
- Monitor suspicious activities
- Verify account legitimacy

### 3. Reviewing Court Status
Admins monitor active courts for ongoing compliance.

**Status Updates**:
- **Approved**: Court actively listing and accepting bookings
- **Under Maintenance**: Owner marked court temporarily unavailable
- **Rejected**: Court not approved for listing
- **Suspended**: Court removed due to policy violation or safety issues

### 4. Platform Statistics
Admins view key metrics about platform performance:
- Total active courts
- Total registered users (players and owners)
- Total bookings made
- Revenue metrics
- User growth rates
- Peak usage times
- Facility type distribution
- Geographic distribution of courts

## Approving Court Listings

### Understanding Court Submission
When an owner submits a court for approval:
- Court information is compiled
- Photos are submitted
- Operating hours provided
- Pricing established
- Description written
- Status set to "Pending Approval"

### Detailed Review Checklist

**Court Information**:
- ✅ Name is clear and descriptive
- ✅ Location is specific and verifiable
- ✅ Court type is specified (badminton, squash, etc.)
- ✅ Hourly rate is realistic for region
- ✅ Operating hours are reasonable

**Owner Information**:
- ✅ Owner account is verified
- ✅ Business appears legitimate
- ✅ Contact information is valid
- ✅ Owner has accepted platform terms

**Court Details**:
- ✅ Photos show actual court condition
- ✅ Description matches photos
- ✅ No safety concerns apparent
- ✅ Facility appears well-maintained
- ✅ Amenities listed are accurate

**Policy Compliance**:
- ✅ Pricing doesn't violate guidelines
- ✅ No prohibited activities mentioned
- ✅ Court is legal and licensed (if required)
- ✅ No discriminatory policies
- ✅ Cancellation policy is fair

### Approval Decisions

**Approve**: If all criteria met
- Court appears immediately in listings
- Owner notified of approval
- Court available for player bookings
- Admin notes recorded

**Reject**: If issues found
- Court not listed
- Owner notified with specific reasons
- Owner given option to resubmit with corrections
- Reason documented in system

**Request Changes**: If minor issues
- Owner asked to fix specific items
- Examples: Update photos, clarify description, adjust hours
- Owner resubmits revised information
- Admin reviews again

## Managing User Complaints and Disputes

### Receiving Complaints
Users may report:
- Facility doesn't match description
- Owner unresponsive to issues
- Safety or maintenance concerns
- Unfair booking practices
- Inappropriate behavior
- Payment disputes

### Investigating Issues
When receiving a complaint:
1. Document complaint details
2. Review booking and communication history
3. Contact involved parties for their version
4. Review court photos and description
5. Check relevant booking records
6. Assess severity and impact

### Taking Action
**Minor Issues**:
- Contact owner to remedy
- Request response to player
- Monitor for improvement
- Follow up if issue continues

**Moderate Issues**:
- Formal warning to owner
- Required improvements by deadline
- Court may be temporarily delisted until fixed
- Monitor compliance

**Serious Issues**:
- Investigation becomes priority
- Court may be suspended
- Owner may be required to make significant improvements
- Repeated violations can result in permanent removal

## Monitoring Platform Activity

### Activity Logs
Admins can view:
- User login times and activities
- Booking creation and modifications
- Payment transactions
- Court listing changes
- User complaints and reports
- System errors and issues

### Suspicious Activity Detection
Watch for:
- Unusual booking patterns
- Rapid account creation
- Multiple accounts from same person
- Fraudulent payments
- Abusive communication
- Spam or marketing violations

### Taking Corrective Action
- Contact user to clarify
- Disable accounts if fraud suspected
- Report to payment processor if applicable
- Escalate to management if serious
- Document all actions taken

## Handling Urgent Issues

### Emergency Procedures
For critical issues:
- Safety hazards (unsafe facility)
- Fraudulent activity
- Data breaches
- System failures
- Severe policy violations

**Response Protocol**:
1. Immediately assess severity
2. Take action to protect users if needed
3. Disable courts or accounts if required
4. Notify management/relevant parties
5. Document incident thoroughly
6. Implement corrective measures

### Court Suspension
If a court poses safety risk or violates policies:
1. Remove from active listings
2. Notify owner immediately
3. Specify reasons for suspension
4. Give timeline for corrections
5. Clarify reinstatement conditions
6. Monitor for compliance

## Preventing Fraud and Abuse

### Fraud Detection
Watch for:
- Fake court listings (scams)
- Stolen payment information
- Money laundering
- Undisclosed discrimination
- Unauthorized price gouging

### Taking Preventive Action
- Verify new owner information thoroughly
- Monitor payment transactions
- Review court photos for authenticity
- Validate owner business legitimacy
- Check for patterns of complaints

### Responding to Fraud
- Disable accounts immediately
- Refund affected users
- Report to authorities if applicable
- Update fraud detection systems
- Notify affected users
- Review preventive measures
